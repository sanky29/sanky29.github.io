I am a first-year PhD student in the School of Artificial Intelligence at Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, co-advised by Parag Singla and Rohan Paul. I graduated with a BTech in Textile Technology and an MTech in Computer Science Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi. I was fortunate to be advised by Parag Singla during my master’s.
I am broadly interested in human-centric machine learning. I believe that a way to build generalized artificial intelligence is to make use of human-centric biases. One of the problems towards human-centric AI is how to segregate the dense neural information in meaningful full representation aka binding problem. I have been working on an object-centric binding problem. I also plan to work on human-centric RL.
